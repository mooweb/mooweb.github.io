This pilot program is designed to help students develop professional skills through a collaboration with tech employees at Mutual of Omaha. Students will work with mentors from Mutual of Omaha to develop web based tools/apps to address an issue they see in the community. This program includes:
  • Tech Careers Exploration Fair
    • May 2 (local employers)
  • Soft skills training (May 10 at Omaha South)
  • Community project development period (May 6 – June 28)
  • Demo day presentation (June 28)

By the end of the program (June 28, 2019), selected student groups will build a website or an application prototype to address an existing community issue with support from mentors on the tech team at Mutual of Omaha. Project ideas must be researched prior to submitting an application to ensure the challenge/issue exists and similar solutions are not in place. If similar solutions are in place, the student team's project idea must include an innovative approach to tackle the issue. Click here to view the project requirements.

Project Dates:
  • Selected projects announcement: May 2, 2019 - COMPLETE!
  • Soft Skills Training: May 10 - COMPLETE!
  • Project Development with Support: May 6 – June 28 - IN PROGRESS
  • Demo Day Presentation and Winners Announcement: June 28
Application Details: Applications open through April 26, 2019

Applications for the summer camp will be open through April 26, 2019. One application per term is required and a team captain is required for each team. Each student can only be a member of one selected project team. If you are part of a team for multiple project submissions and more than one of those projects is selected, you must commit to one team for the duration of this project. You are welcome to help other teams, but can only be formally listed with one team. Click here to view the application.

Bring Your A+ Game Training: May 10 8:30a - 2:55p at Omaha South

During this required session led by Metropolitan Community College in collaboration with Heartland Workforce Solutions, students selected to participate in the project will learn essential soft skills for the workplace. This training session will be held at Omaha South High School. Skills students will learn include:

  • Professionalism
  • Relational Skills
  • Conflict Resolution
  • Taking Direction
Following this 6-hour workshop, you will have learned the skills to earn the Certificate of Work Ethic Proficiency.

Project Support Period: May 6 - June 28 (online or in person)

During this timeframe, students will work independently to build their prototypes in preparation for the Demo Day event on June 28, 2019. Required dates during this timeframe follow:

  • Attend Bring Your A+ Game Training on May 10 at Omaha South
  • Project Timeline Due May 17

    • The project timeline should include high level plans to complete the project including:

    • Dates and times you will meet with your mentor(s).
    • Date your product design will be complete.
    • Demo dates for your product (recommend 3 demos during the 6-week project with your team mentor).
    • Date for your final product demo (due by June 21).
    • Demo Day preparation dates.
  • Product Design Complete by May 24
  • Final Product Demo with Mentor(s) by June 21
  • Demo Day on June 28

Demo Day! June 28, 2019 – location TBD, 6p – 8p

During the final event for the summer pilot program, student teams will present their products to an audience of family, friends, teachers, mentors, etc. Each presentation will be judged by a panel of Greater Omaha based community leaders. Projects will be judged based on the following criteria:

  • Ambition of project
  • Innovation of solution
  • Quality of implementation
  • Quality of presentation
  • Team collaboration

The winning project team will receive a $500 stipend, 2nd place: $250, 3rd place: $100. All students who complete the entire project based on the requirements listed here who also present on Demo Day will receive a $250 stipend.

Team Collaboration Requirements

On a weekly basis, all team members will submit a simple survey on the team’s performance. Survey submissions are a required part of the project and will help organizers ensure that all team members are contributing to the success of the project throughout the development timeline.
